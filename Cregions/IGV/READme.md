# Hamster 10x 3' GEX Data Alignment to Reference Genome
This folder contains alignment visualizations from **Integrative Genome Viewer (IGV)** of **10x 3' GEX data** from Syrian hamsters used by *Oliver et al.* for Ig/TCR C-region consensus sequence generation. These reads were aligned to the hamster reference genome (**GCF_017639785.1**) using the **STAR** aligner.

The **Ig/TCR constant region annotation track** shown in the figures is based on the annotations generated by *Adams et al. (2025)*, which shows the *IGHG3* constant region structure. Notably, *Oliver et al.* reported that *IGHG3* harbors an intron retention event between exons 2 and 3 (CH2 region). This analysis was conducted to assess read support for that event using 10x 3' GEX data.

Our findings indicate that there is **no read support for the intron retention event** proposed by *Oliver et al.*, thereby supporting the *Adams et al. (2025)* *IGHG3* exon-intron splicing pattern. The absence of read evidence suggests that the intron retention is likely an assembly error.

## Figures

![Uninfected Hamster Tissues](https://github.com/ncsu-penglab/HamsterIgTCR/blob/main/Cregions/IGV/AllHamsters_Uninfected.png)

**Figure 1.Alignment of uninfected hamster tissues to reference genome.** This figure shows the alignment of **10x 3' GEX data** from **uninfected tissues** of all hamsters to the reference genome at the *IGHG3* locus. While there is minimal read support for *IGHG3* across these samples, **no evidence of intron retention between exons 2 and 3 (CH2 region)** is observed. This support the **Adams et al. **Figure 2.Alignment of infected hamster #1 tissues from all timepoints to reference genome.** This figure presents the **infected tissues from hamster 1** at multiple timepoints post SARS-CoV-2 infection. Although some **read support for *IGHG3*** is observed at **14dpi in lung tissue**, there remains **no evidence of intron retention** between exons 2 and 3 in any condition. This further supports the **Adams et al. (2025)** annotation and suggests that the proposed intron retention by *Oliver et al.* is likely an assembly error.

---

## Conclusion
This analysis demonstrates that **10x 3' GEX data** from both **uninfected** and **infected** Syrian hamster tissues **do not support the intron retention event** within the CH2 region of *IGHG3* as described by *Oliver et al.*. Instead, the data supports with the *Adams et al. (2025)* C-region exon-intron pattern, reinforcing that the intron retention is likely an **assembly error**.
